🚨 EOD Alert — Overnight Risk: Doha Talks Stalled, Hormuz Flow Still Frozen

What happened at the close: Dow closed at a record 52,319.20 (+0.26%), S&P 500 at 7,449.36 (+0.79%), Nasdaq Composite at 26,213.72 (+1.52%) — the best quarter for the S&P and Nasdaq since the 2020 rebound. Tech led (+2.38%), Comcast popped 25% premarket on its NBCUniversal/Sky spin-off, and Nike beat on EPS (helped by a one-time tariff-recovery benefit). The tape closed the quarter looking like an all-clear.

What the market expected vs. reality: Crude sold off into the close (WTI −0.7% to $70.2, Brent −1% to $72.4) on hopes that today's US-Iran talks in Doha would de-escalate the conflict. But Iran's Foreign Ministry said this morning it won't return to the table until the US forces Israel to halt operations in Lebanon — a precondition that has not been met. Maritime traffic through the Strait of Hormuz remains down roughly 95% for crude and 99% for LNG since the air war began in late February, and weekend attacks on shipping only tightened that chokepoint further.

Watch overnight: The 60-day Iran oil-export waiver from the June 17 interim deal is still running, but the underlying conflict is not resolved. Any breakdown in Doha, a new strike, or a CENTCOM statement can gap CL violently before tomorrow's open. Do not hold short crude overnight. Separately, today closed Q2 — tomorrow opens Q3, and the window-dressing bid that lifted tech into the close evaporates with the new quarter. Expect a volatile first hour on July 1.
