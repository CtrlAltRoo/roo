EOD ALERT — OVERNIGHT RISK: HORMUZ ESCALATION + US STRIKES ON IRAN

What happened at the close: Wall Street closed lower Tuesday — S&P 500 -0.45% to 7,503.85, Nasdaq Composite -1.16% to 25,818.69, while the Dow held up better, -0.25% to 52,925.15 (still near record territory). The drag came from a second straight day of semiconductor selling — Micron, KLA, Marvell, Broadcom, and AMD all fell, with the VanEck Semiconductor ETF (SMH) down more than 3% as Samsung's results and reports that China's DeepSeek is developing its own AI chip renewed doubt about AI-demand fundamentals. Sector rotation was stark: Energy led with +3.02%, Healthcare gained +1.55%, while Technology dropped -1.62%.

The bigger story broke after the cash close: Iran struck the Qatari LNG carrier Al-Rekayyat and other vessels transiting the Strait of Hormuz. In response, U.S. Central Command said it began launching "a series of powerful strikes" on Iran, and the Treasury Department revoked the waiver that had allowed Iran to sell oil and petrochemicals under the recent interim deal. Brent popped 5.6% in after-hours trade to $76.04 and WTI jumped 5.4% to $72.25 — a much sharper move than the daytime session showed. The Joint Maritime Information Center has the Hormuz threat level at "SEVERE."

Overnight hold warning: This is a live, developing military and energy-market event happening after the close, not a rumor. Any further Iranian retaliation, a shipping disruption, or a broadened U.S. response overnight can gap CL, GC, and defense names sharply at Wednesday's open while pressuring an already-fragile NQ. No index-long holds overnight; energy and gold exposure should be sized for gap risk, not held with wide stops.
