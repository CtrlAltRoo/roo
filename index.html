EOD ALERT — OVERNIGHT RISK

Equities closed sharply higher — a chip-led risk-on session that snapped a three-day losing streak (NQ futures reclaimed the high-28,900s, S&P 500 +0.89% to 7,509, Dow +0.74% to 52,225). But the rally happened in spite of, not because of, the tape's biggest driver: the U.S.–Iran conflict entered its tenth day, an Iranian tanker was hit in the Strait of Hormuz, and vessel traffic through the corridor that moves ~20% of the world's oil has effectively stalled. Crude spiked again (WTI +2.2% to $84.29, Brent +2.1% to $91.10) and both metals ripped (gold +1.75% to $4,083, silver +4.48% to $58.95).

Why this is an overnight-risk night, not a confirmed all-clear: stocks priced earnings and semis while ignoring a live shooting war over a closed oil chokepoint. That is a classic divergence — the equity narrative and the commodity/geopolitical narrative are pointed in opposite directions into the close. Any headline overnight (tanker escalation, a Houthi strike on the ~2.5M bpd of Saudi supply reportedly at risk, or a ceasefire surprise) gaps equities and energy hard in either direction.

Overnight hold warning: Hormuz is a headline-driven gap engine right now. Carrying index or energy risk through tonight's Globex session is a bet on a coin flip you can't manage. Day traders should be flat into the close and reassess at the 7/22 pre-market.
